# TASK 4 REPORT – Email & Social Media Etiquette

## Part A – Professional Emails
For Part A, I drafted two professional emails to practice effective digital communication. The first email was addressed to a professor, requesting an extension on an assignment submission deadline. It included a clear subject line, a polite greeting, a concise explanation of the reason for the request, and a professional closing. The second email was directed to an internship coordinator, expressing interest in a summer internship opportunity. This email highlighted my skills, academic background, and enthusiasm for gaining practical experience.  

Completing these emails helped me understand the importance of tone, clarity, and proper structure in professional correspondence. I learned how small details, such as subject lines and courteous phrasing, can influence how a message is received. Practicing these skills is essential for both academic and career communication.

## Part B – Social Media Do’s and Don’ts
For Part B, I created a checklist of 5 Do’s and 5 Don’ts for responsible social media use. This exercise highlighted the importance of thoughtful online behavior. For example, a hypothetical scenario involved a student sharing confidential exam materials online, which led to an academic integrity investigation. Using privacy settings and careful judgment before posting could have prevented this issue.  

This task reinforced that professional email etiquette and responsible social media use are both critical aspects of a student’s digital literacy. Being mindful of online communication ensures credibility, safety, and a positive digital presence.
