## Part A – Case Study: Phishing Attack 📧
For Part A, I researched phishing attacks, a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. Phishing typically happens in the following steps:  

1. A victim receives an email, SMS, or WhatsApp message appearing to be from a trusted entity, such as a bank or government portal.  
2. The message uses urgent language, like “Your account will be blocked” or “Immediate action required ⚠️.”  
3. A link in the message redirects the victim to a fake website that looks identical to the legitimate platform.  
4. The victim enters sensitive information, which the attacker then collects.  
5. The attacker uses this data for financial theft, identity theft, or unauthorized transactions.  

Students and young adults are often targeted due to their high online activity and frequent use of digital payment platforms. Consequences include financial loss, identity theft, account compromise, and long-term security issues. Recovery usually involves reporting the incident, changing credentials, and sometimes legal action.  

## Part B – Prevention Checklist 🛡️
For Part B, I created a “Stay Safe Online” checklist with 10 actionable tips for students:  
- Never click on suspicious links from messages or emails.  
- Verify sender addresses before responding.  
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all accounts.  
- Do not share UPI PINs, OTPs, or banking passwords. *(Financial Safety Tip)*  
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for financial transactions. *(Financial Safety Tip)*  
- Use strong, unique passwords for each platform.  
- Keep devices and antivirus software updated.  
- Limit personal information shared on social media.  
- Report phishing or suspicious activity to the [National Cyber Crime Portal](https://cybercrime.gov.in) or Helpline 1930.  
- Educate yourself and peers about common online scams.  

## Reflection 💭
What surprised me most was how easily attackers exploit trust and urgency to trick victims, even with minimal technical skills. Personally, I will now always verify links and enable two-factor authentication on every online account to reduce my risk of falling victim to phishing or other cybercrimes.
