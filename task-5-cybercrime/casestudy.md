# Case Study: Phishing Attack 📧

## What is Phishing?  
Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords, bank account details, or personal data, by pretending to be a trustworthy entity. Phishing often exploits trust, urgency, or fear to manipulate victims into taking immediate action without thinking.

## How it Happens (Step-by-Step) 🔍
1. The victim receives an email, SMS, or WhatsApp message appearing to be from a bank, government portal, or popular online service.  
2. The message often contains urgent language, such as “Your account will be blocked” or “Immediate action required ⚠️.”  
3. A link in the message redirects the victim to a fake website that looks almost identical to the legitimate platform.  
4. The victim unknowingly enters login credentials, UPI PINs, or other sensitive data on the fake website.  
5. The attacker collects this information for financial theft, identity theft, or unauthorized transactions.

## Who is Targeted? 🎯  
Phishing can target anyone using digital platforms, but students and young adults are particularly vulnerable due to high online activity, online banking, and reliance on digital payment apps.

## Consequences ⚠️  
Victims may face financial loss, unauthorized transactions, identity theft, and long-term issues like compromised accounts or blackmail. Recovery typically involves reporting the crime, changing all credentials, monitoring accounts, and sometimes pursuing legal action.  

**Note:** This scenario is based on common phishing incidents reported in India and emphasizes the importance of vigilance, strong passwords, and verifying digital communications before taking action.
